How to help


YOU can help give girls around the world the education they deserve by visiting the following charity and campaign sites, all of which invest in girls for a brighter future. Donations are used to support education initiatives by providing textbooks and other learning materials or furnishing classrooms, as well as providing access to safe education, sanitation and healthcare. Sponsoring a girl can ensure that girls are protected from child labour and early marriage, and some charities offer scholarships so that girls can be the first in their generation to go to and complete school.


CAMFED
Camfed (Campaign for Female Education) was
founded in 1993 and has the mission to eradicate poverty in Africa through the education
of girls and young women.
MALALA FUND
The Malala Fund was formed in 2013 and founded
by Malala Yousafzai. The organisation hopes to ensure that every girl has 12 years
of safe, quality education.
GIRLS NOT BRIDES
Girls Not Brides is a charity that aims to put a stop
to child marriage. Girls Not Brides aims to combat
child marriage with education.
SHE'S THE FIRST
She's The First is an award winning oragnisation that
fights gender inequality through education. They provide
scholarships to make girls the first in their families to graduate from high school.
PLAN INTERNATIONAL: GIRLS GET EQUAL
Plan International's global campaign Girls Get Equal
aims to "demand power, freedom and respect for girls
and young women".
MERCY CORPS: A GIRL CAN
Mercy Corps' A Girl Can campaign create safe learning
spaces, provide job training and help girls stay in school.
SAVE THE CHILDREN
Save the Children is an organisation that promotes
children's rights. They aim to reach the most vulnerable
children missing out on education, such as girls.
UNICEF
UNICEF (The United Nation's International Children's
Emergency Fund) provides aid for emergencies. They're
also trying to remove the barriers to education by providing
supplies and much more.
ONE GIRL
One Girl is an organisation with the aim of educating
1 million girls and putting an end to practices like child marriage which prevent girls
from receiving an education.
